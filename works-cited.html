§ III — Data & Methods Note
On the Data

1876 data. The survey was managed by Commissioner of Education John Eaton, with data collection beginning in 1870 to document new libraries, their collections, and a guide to their holdings. The report also included Charles Cutter's cataloging rules, Melvil Dewey's classification schema, and the prospectus for the American Library Association. Statistician Mary E. McLellan is credited for compiling the statistical tables used in this analysis. PDFs were downloaded from HathiTrust, cleaned, cropped, and fed to Gemini 3.1 Pro with prompts requesting structured JSON output; outputs were spot-checked against original pages.


Today's data. Contemporary data combines the 2023 Public Library Survey and 2024 Integrated Postsecondary Education Data System, covering public branches, bookmobiles, and academic libraries. Unlike the 1876 survey, modern federal efforts no longer collect data on private, subscription, military, government, or carceral libraries.


Geocoding. Historic library locations were geocoded via Nominatim / OpenStreetMap contributors. Built with Leaflet.js, Chart.js, and D3.js.


Future work includes replicating this workflow with open-source models, recovering 20th-century founding dates from Bowker's 1989 Library Index, linking historic institutions to contemporary federal identifiers, and overlaying the dataset with historical maps of railroads, schools, and postal expansion.

Data: Public Libraries in the United States of America, Bureau of Education, 1876. Geocoding via Nominatim / OpenStreetMap contributors. Built with Leaflet.js & Chart.js.
